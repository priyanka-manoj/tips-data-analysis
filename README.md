

# Tips Dataset - Exploratory Data Analysis

##  Problem Statement
Analyze trends and patterns in restaurant revenue and tipping behavior across different customer segments.

---

## Dataset
The dataset contains 244 restaurant transactions with features such as:
- total_bill
- tip
- sex (bill payer)
- smoker status
- day of the week (Thu, Fri, Sat, Sun)
- time (lunch/dinner)
- group size

---

##  Key Insights

### Overall Patterns
- Total bill and tip show a clear positive relationship
- Most bills fall in the range of 10–30 USD

### Tipping Behaviour
- Most customers tip ~10–20%
- Tip % is highly variable for small bills but stabilizes for larger bills

### Customer Segments
- No significant difference in tipping behaviour across gender or smoker status
- No consistent variation across days
- Dinner generates ~3x more revenue than lunch

### Group Size Impact
- Larger groups generate higher total bills
- Total bill is the primary driver of tip

---

##  Note:
- This is an exploratory analysis on a small dataset (n=244)
